<span class="newthought">I practise as a barrister</span> specialising in intellectual property, technology law, and commercial disputes. My work brings me into contact with software companies, creative industries, academic institutions, and the occasional very surprised individual who has just discovered that the internet does not forget.

## Practice areas

My principal areas of practice are:

- **Intellectual property** — copyright, trade marks, passing off, design rights, and database rights
- **Technology law** — software licensing, open source compliance, AI and authorship, platform liability
- **Data protection** — GDPR compliance, enforcement, and litigation
- **Commercial** — contracts, confidentiality, and disputes where the subject-matter happens to be intangible

[^1] I have a particular interest in the intersection of IP law and emerging technology — a space that generates an unusual number of questions to which nobody yet knows the answer.

[^1]: This includes questions such as: who owns a work generated by an AI system? Can a database right subsist in a machine-learning training set? Does a terms-of-service clause preclude a fair dealing defence? I find these questions genuinely interesting, which is either a professional asset or a personality flaw.

## This site

[^ {-} The site is built with Eleventy and styled with Tufte CSS. Source on GitHub.]

Writing here covers law, technology, and the places they meet. Posts range from close analysis of recent decisions to broader reflection on where the law is heading. I try to write for a reader who is intelligent but not necessarily legally trained.

Nothing here is legal advice. If you have a legal problem, you need a lawyer — ideally one who has read the relevant cases more recently than I have.
